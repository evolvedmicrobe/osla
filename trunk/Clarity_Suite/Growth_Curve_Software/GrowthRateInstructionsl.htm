Performing a Growth Rate Experiment

 

            This page will allow you to easily setup a protocol to measure growth rates.  There is really only one part of this that you must not screw up.  At some point you will be asked to select the slots where the plates whose growth rate you want to measure are located.  Only select your own plates and make sure that there is a plate in the slot.  Failure to do so will crash your protocol and is very likely to tick someone off (no one likes their plate unloaded all the time as part of some other protocol, and if you tell Clarity to expect a plate in an incubator slot and it doesn’t find one, it assumes that it has screwed up and will throw an error).  With that one caution, here are the simple steps to create a growth rate protocol.

 

1-      Go to the incubator controls tab and load your plates

Place a plate on the transfer station, make sure the well labeled A1 is facing towards the twister.  Next select an empty slot from the “Select Plate Slot” list box where you would like to place your plate.  The slots are numbered 1-44, and the corresponding numbers are written on the incubator (where two numbers are written, eg 39/17 use the higher number).  Once you have selected the slot, hit the “Load Plate” button.  Repeat for all the plates you plan to load, remembering which slots you have selected.

2-      Set your desired shaking speed

From the dropdown box, select a shaking speed.  Then hit the “Change Shaking Speed Button” to set the speed.  Once it is set, hit “Start Shaking.”

3-      Go Back to This Panel And Fill In The Required Information

a.      Select all of the plate slots whose growth rate you want to measure.

b.      Fill in a minute delay, e.g. 90.  Currently, delays must be over 30 minutes, but if you want this changed please ask.

c.       Set the number of times to take measurements.  A protocol with an hour delay measured 48 times will run for 2 days.

d.       Name your experiment, but please keep these names short.  Your data will be output in a directories prefixed with your protocol name.

e.      Type your email address.  Multiple emails should be separated by a semicolon.

f.         Double check everything (particularly the plate slots!) and hit start.

4-      Start your protocol

a.      If other protocols are already running you will be prompted to start your protocol.  Otherwise, go to the “Run Protocols” tab and hit the “Start Protocol Execution” button.
